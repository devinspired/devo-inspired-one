Networking Contributes the Most to Job Search Success
August 26, 2012


Applying for one or two posted jobs may result in a job offer - or not. ‘Networking’ is probably the most useful step in your search, as it has been identified as the factor contributing the most to job search success. This includes “social networking” on sites such as LinkedIn, Facebook, MySpace, Twitter and Pinterest.

A social networking service is a platform or site that focuses on the building of social networks for people who share interests or relationships. Individuals who register create a profile and develop links to a network of people of their choosing. There is no charge for registering on the popular sites. You can share links to your profile with potential employers, and show them that you're up on the latest networking trends. Link up your sites so that someone searching on LinkedIn can also see your Pinterest portfolio; selecting your short Tweet will lead to your LinkedIn profile. There are also site interest groups that you can follow that may provide information and post jobs. Employers use these sites to find candidates by posting jobs or searching for candidates, therefore it is important to register. You can also search yourself for people who may help you, such as someone employed in the same firm where you are applying or the same industry.

To register on these sites just put the name into the Internet address section at the top of your search engine page, hit return and the site name will appear. You can download your resume to most sites, but there are questions to answer and summaries to write too. Employers will be searching using key words for the position they are filling, consequently you need to research the common requirements of your target job to make yourself findable. Ensure that all of the information that you put in your profile is professional. You will want to have the people that you are ‘following’ to have a reasonably respectable background.

LinkedIn will interview you about your education and experience and build a searchable file. It is known as the job search site for professionals. If you're in a creative or design-related field, Pinterest is a great place to build a portfolio without having to develop your own website. You can create a board showcasing your design work or pin your visually-appealing resume. A Facebook or MySpace site can provide insight into your personal goals and values. Also, all those cousins of cousins that you have on your site are part of your referral network – tell them about your job search! Twitter will expose you to millions of people. You can only make short ‘Tweets’ on the network, but you will have a profile on the site and links to your other social networking sites. A recent article said one big value of Twitter is the support that it gives the job searcher.

Some sites have the option of submitting a photograph. Usually there is only room for a small head and shoulders shot. If you do not have a professional looking photo, better to leave it out. This is not a selection requirement - for example, the majority of people do not have photos on LinkedIn. If you use a poor quality photo, it will reflect negatively on the look of your profile. Of course graphics and pictures on the site need to be professional.

Networking is time consuming. Just registering on all of these sites can take days – for me anyway! If you are limited in time and are using a traditional resume approach, I would recommend LinkedIn as the best use of your time in North America. I understand that Pinterest provides the same service for Asia.

Invest time in networking and you probably will be rewarded. You need to have a good resume to get the best out of any job search networking.
